By Shervin Abdolhamidi, Class of 2021

Nightlife in New York City has been decimated by the pandemic, and any creative solutions were hampered after Gov. Andrew Cuomo ordered bars and social gatherings in New York State to close at 10 p.m., on Nov. 11.

These restrictions have led to a rise in illegal raves around the cityâ€”and especially in Brooklyn. Two warehouse parties attended by nearly 1,000 persons were broken up by New York City authorities over Halloween and a 400-person party was shutdown over Thanksgiving weekend.

While Brooklyn has always had a notoriety for illegal raves, NYC Open Data shows a rise in residential noise complaints in 2020, compared to the same period in 2019.

"We have no tolerance for the illegal & reckless endangerment of public health." said Gov. Cuomo, in response to a video released of an illegal party.
